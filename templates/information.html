Transparency

Tips & Tricks for health inside and out

Our data regarding food products comes from "The Great Climate Database." The CO2e that each food product possesses is measured as follows, according to The Great Climate Database: "The climate footprint of food products is stated in kg CO2 equivalents (CO2e) per kg of product (net weight) and expresses the forward-looking global climate consequences of consuming different types of food. CO2e includes the climate impact from the emission of CO2 and other greenhouse gases such as methane and nitrous oxide, as well as the indirect land-use change that food production causes."

The database provides the content of the food per 100g. When you enter the grams you use, we calculate it as follows: "food product * your input in grams / 100," and for CO2e, we calculate it as: "food product * your input / 1000." The reason we divide by 1000 for CO2e is that the climate database states the CO2e of food products in kg, and we want to show it to you in grams.
